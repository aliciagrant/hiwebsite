problem

Dance Marathon at the University of Florida participants needed a way to consolidate managing their fundraising efforts, tracking goals, earning spirit points, and staying updated on events and meetings. The DM at UF app aims to streamline these processes, fostering a more efficient and collaborative experience for its members.

tools

Figma, React Native

details

Duration: 11 weeks
Design Process: 6 weeks
Role: UX Designer
Skills: Prototyping, Figma, Iterative Design

context

The DM at UF app was expected to be designed, developed, and fully functional within just three months to support over 1,500 members, making the project timeline and scalability critical factors in the design process. We began by looking at the types of users that would be using the application.

Miracle Makers

Each year, students stay excited and pledge to try to raise money for Children's Miracle Network Hospitals. The app needed to offer a way to check fundraising totals, earn incentives, and access links fast.

Emerging Leaders / Ambassadors

Students in leadership or ambassador roles needed a platform to stay on top of meetings, check event assignments, and communicate efficiently.

Captains

Captains from marketing, operations, morale, and other teams needed access to event scheduling, point systems, and task coordination across committees.

Directors

Directors required high-level views of the different captain teams, event logistics, and user activity to guide the organization effectively.

emerging designs

With tangible users and their concerns in mind, a base for the application was made. As a captain on the digital marketing team, I presented the rough sketches to my overall director and the directors of other teams and began designing with intent of development.

iterative design

Due to the time crunch and the high expectation of reach that we were expected to meet, I opted out of formal testing and utilized iterative design. Each captain meeting I attended, I had my team go through the prototype, give me feedback, and I would implement what I deemed necessary. This cycle continued until no refinements were immediately necessary. With my designs, the developers on my team created and launched DM at UF.
